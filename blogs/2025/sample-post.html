What are Score-based Generative Models?

Score-based generative models are a class of generative models that use score matching to learn the gradient of the log probability density function of the data distribution. This approach has gained significant attention in recent years due to its impressive performance in generating high-quality samples.

Key Concepts

The score function is defined as the gradient of the log probability density function:

\[ \nabla_x \log p(x) \]

In score-based generative models, we typically:

  1. Train a neural network to estimate the score function
  2. Use Langevin dynamics to sample from the learned distribution

Applications in Fashion

At Zalando, we've been exploring applications of score-based generative models for fashion image generation. Some potential applications include:

  • Virtual try-on
  • Outfit completion
  • Style transfer
